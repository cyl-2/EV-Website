Who is Arrival?

Arrival is a London-based electric vehicle company in the UK, founded in 2015. Arrival differentiates itself from other EV manufacturers as it specializes in zero-emission electric delivery vans and buses, with an ambitious vision to make cities cleaner and more efficient places to live.
Today, Arrival has grown to a team of 1200+ people across 11 cities, in 8 countries around the world. A company backed by Hyundai, Kia and UPS, Arrival shows a strong future in the EV market as their vehicles will be rolled out for use throughout Europe in the fourth quarter of 2021.

Introducing the Arrival bus, the EV bus you wouldn’t mind hours waiting for

This futuristic looking bus is very attractive and modern. I personally, certainly, wouldn’t mind waiting for THIS bus in the freezing Irish weather. The bus will be priced the same as an equivalent diesel bus, according to the company, this price tag makes Arrival a very appealing new choice to bus operators.

The Arrival Bus

The Arrival bus is designed to promote a positive perception of public transport and create an environment evoking a sense of space and cleanliness, which, without a doubt, is very much needed in our world today as we very likely have to continue to live with Covid-19 for the next few years.

Low cost, low emission, high-quality production

Every Arrival vehicle will be manufactured in microfactories, which are small factories that serve one particular city. Microfactories makes use of existing commercial spaces, sourcing from the local area and producing vehicles to meet local demand. By producing vehicles in this manner, the production costs will be significantly lower and it will also help to reduce the environmental impact of manufacturing.
