What are electric vehicles?

Electric vehicles are powered by electricity, which drastically reduces fuel consumption for transportation. By driving an electric powered vehicle, you are reducing the amount of harmful gases released into the atmosphere, causing global climate change. Furthermore, you get to save on costs, as EVs are cheaper than petrol or diesel-fuelled vehicles.
On top of that, the Irish government is set to ban new petrol and diesel cars by 2030 in hopes to achieve our emission targets. You can read more about the ban here. With that said, isn’t it smart to start looking for the next EV to purchase right now?

Some Electric Vehicle Facts

  • Most EVs have a built-in sound when they are moving at lower speeds. This alerts pedestrians to their presence.
  • There are zero harmful gas emissions produced from EVs. Transport accounts for one third of Ireland’s energy related CO2 emissions, by switching to EVs, we can make a huge difference in our carbon footprint.
  • Evs are cheaper to buy as there are grants and reductions for every purchase of an EV.
  • EVs are at the lowest tax band for vehicle road tax (which is calculated on the emissions level of the vehicle), because EVs are zero-emission.
  • EVs are cheaper to service and there is less wear and tear as EVs don’t have engine oils and fewer moving parts.
  • EVs are cheaper to fuel, one can save as much as 70% on fuel costs!
  • A research done by the European Energy Agency found that, even with electricity generation, the carbon emissions of an electric car are still 17 – 30% lower than driving a petrol or diesel car.
  • The SEAI (Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland) offers grants of up to €10, 000 on EVs purchased and registered in Ireland.
